FCA DISP — EXTENDED COMPLAINTS RULES AND COMPENSATION
Source: FCA Handbook DISP, Financial Ombudsman Service Rules

FINANCIAL OMBUDSMAN SERVICE — COMPENSATION LIMITS
The Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) can consider complaints
about most financial matters including banking, insurance,
investments, pensions, and credit.

For complaints referred to the FOS on or after 1 April 2024,
the maximum compensation award is £415,000.

For complaints referred between 1 April 2019 and 31 March 2024,
the maximum award was £375,000.

The FOS award limit applies to the financial loss element
of a complaint. The FOS may also award compensation for
distress and inconvenience separately.

COMPLAINT RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS
Under DISP 1.9, firms must:
(1) keep a record of each complaint received for a minimum
period of three years from the date the complaint was received
(2) keep records of all measures taken for resolution of
each complaint
(3) make complaint records available to the FCA upon request
(4) retain records in a durable medium that allows retrieval

The three year retention period applies to all complaints
from eligible complainants regardless of outcome.

For MiFID business, the retention period is five years.

ELIGIBLE COMPLAINANTS
An eligible complainant is:
(1) a consumer — any individual acting for purposes outside
their trade, business or profession
(2) a micro-enterprise — a business with annual turnover
less than two million euros and fewer than ten employees
(3) a charity with annual income less than one million pounds
(4) a trustee of a trust with net assets less than one
million pounds
(5) a small business with annual turnover less than six
and a half million pounds

COMPLAINT HANDLING PROCESS — FULL TIMELINE
Day 0: Complaint received
Day 3: Firm must send Summary Resolution Communication
       if complaint resolved within 3 business days
Day 5: Written acknowledgement must be sent if not resolved
Week 8: Final response must be sent in all cases

If the firm does not send a final response within eight weeks,
the complainant may refer their complaint to the FOS
regardless of whether the firm has responded.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS REQUIREMENTS
Firms must carry out root cause analysis of complaints.
This means firms must:
(1) analyse complaints to identify the root cause
(2) consider whether root causes affect other customers
who have not complained
(3) take proactive steps to remedy issues for all
affected customers, not just those who complained
(4) document root cause analysis findings
(5) report significant root cause findings to senior
management and the board

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS AND CONSUMER DUTY
Under the Consumer Duty, root cause analysis has become
more important. Firms must use complaint data as one
source of evidence when assessing whether they are
delivering good outcomes for retail customers.

Complaint patterns that suggest poor outcomes must be
escalated to the Consumer Duty board champion.

SUMMARY RESOLUTION COMMUNICATION
A Summary Resolution Communication must:
(1) refer to the fact that the complainant has made a
complaint and state that the firm now considers it resolved
(2) inform the complainant that if they remain dissatisfied
they may refer their complaint to the FOS
(3) provide the website address of the FOS
(4) refer to the availability of the FOS consumer leaflet
